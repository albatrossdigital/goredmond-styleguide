Commute Trip Reduction Laws

The Washington State Legislature passed the Commute Trip  Reduction (CTR) Law in 1991. Some Redmond employers are affected by the following regulations. The City of Redmond adopted the law into the municipal code, which is located in Chapter 5.65 under business licenses and regulations [pdf].

Is my business affected?

Employers are affected by the CTR Law if you have 100 or more full-time employees at a single worksite who begin their workday between 6:00 am and 9:00 am at least two days a week.

Can my company participate if we are not affected by the CTR Law?

Yes! The City of Redmond encourages all Redmond employers to participate in a commute reduction program, regardless if you are required by law. Companies not affected by the law have all the same access to tools and services available to promote commute alteratives.

I am newly affected by CTR law. Now what do I do?             

The CTR law requires employers that meet the definition of an affected employerto identify themselves to the local city/county within 180 days of either moving into the boundaries of the jurisdiction or growing in employment to 100 or more employees.

Minimal Requirements

CTR-affected employers are required to:

  • Assign an Employee Transportation Coordinator (ETC) to manage the commute program
  • Survey employees every two years to assess progress toward CTR goals.
  • Submit an annual employer program report - Due each November 1st
  • Act in good faith to reduce drive-alone commute trips

Transportation Management Program (TMP)

A Transportation Management Program (TMP) is  a condition of new commercial development. (Redmond Zoning Code 21.52.020.)   Each TMP contains a performance goal, survey and reporting requirements, along with a set of elements to assist in achieving and maintaining the development's performance level. These elements include:

  • A Transportation Coordinator
  • Marketing and Promotion of the program
  • Preferential parking for carpools and vanpools
  • Transportation Information Centers
  • Membership in a Transportation Management Association
  • Incentives (at least one incentive equivalent to a two-zone peak period transit pass)

At least one additional financial incentive or equivalent program identified as a contingency measure in the event that target goals are not met.

Property owners may also propose a demonstrated, equally effective alternative in lieu of a required element. Such substitution is subject to approval by the City's Technical Committee.

R-TRIP can assist property owners, employers, and tenants in meeting TMP performance goals by providing program administration capabilities through the Commute Management System, starter incentives and subsidies, and marketing and other resource assistance.
