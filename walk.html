Walk

“All truly great thoughts are conceived while walking.” ― Friedrich Nietzsche
Even though over half of car trips in the United States are less than 1 mile (or about a 20-minute walk), we still hop in the car for many of these short trips. 

Luckily, Redmond is a "Walkers Paradise" making it easy for you to swap some of these short drives for a stroll. Even if you can’t walk for your commute, the benefits of walking to grab a bite to eat or to run your errands are hard to beat!

Benefits of Walking

  • 30 minutes of walking reduces risk of heart disease and certain cancers
  • Walking has been shown to be a mood booster and stress reducer
  • FitBit & Microsoft Band bragging rights

Where to Walk in Redmond

Think Redmond Directory of Local Businesses

Pull up Think Redmond on your mobile phone to find local Redmond stores, restaurants, and services that are near your location! You can get walking and biking directions right on your phone, and many businesses offer discounts to Think Redmond users.

Downtown Redmond

Downtown Redmond has a WalkScore of 94 out of a 100, a “Walkers Paradise.” With abundant  trails-like the Redmond Central Connector and Sammamish River Trail-and uber connected neighborhoods, walking just makes sense.  

Overlake Neighborhood

Overlake is one of Redmond's urban centers and it is growing and changing quickly! It's still a great place for a stroll.  Check out the walking map of Overlake and Grasslawn neighborhoods here.

Incentives for People Who Walk

  • $50 after your first 50 trips logged on your Commute Calendar
  • Entry into monthly prize drawings
  • Safety gear, such as reflectors and flashers, available under "Rewards"
